Who’s behind Phrases Media?

Phrases Media was created by Nathan Frazer, a Senior Network Operations Coordinator based in Australia. Nathan works in radio and broadcast operations, where a lot of the job is solving practical problems under time pressure and supporting people who just want things to “work”.

That same mindset sits behind these tools. Each one started as a small idea to solve a specific problem: understanding redundancy payouts, planning parental leave, mapping out a process, or making rosters easier to read. None of them are meant to be complicated – they’re designed so that anyone can open the page and understand what to do in a few seconds.

What is Phrases Media trying to do?

The goal is simple: build small, focused tools that save people time and reduce stress. Instead of huge “all-in-one” systems, Phrases Media focuses on single-purpose apps that do one job well. That might be:

  • Helping someone see how long a redundancy payout could cover their mortgage.
  • Helping new parents understand their income while they’re on leave.
  • Turning a list of steps into a clean flowchart automatically.
  • Highlighting pay days in a roster at a glance.

How are the tools built?

Most of the tools are lightweight web apps or browser extensions. They are designed to run quickly, use clear language, and avoid unnecessary clutter. The focus is on:

  • Plain English explanations.
  • Simple inputs and clear outputs.
  • Layouts that work on both desktop and mobile.

Phrases Media will continue to grow as new tools are created. The idea is to keep things practical, honest, and genuinely useful for everyday life.
