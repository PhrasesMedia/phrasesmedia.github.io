Current Tools

Redundancy Ray

Redundancy Ray helps people estimate their redundancy payout and understand how far it might go. It can show a simple breakdown of payments and give a rough idea of how long a payout could support mortgage repayments. It’s designed for Australians who want a clear view during a stressful time.

Open Redundancy Ray →

BabyPay

BabyPay is for parents planning time off work. It brings together government parental leave, employer-paid leave, and estimated after-tax income, so families can see what their income might look like over the months around a new baby. The goal is to make it easier to plan ahead with fewer surprises.

Open BabyPay →

Simple Flow

Simple Flow turns a list of steps into a flowchart automatically. As you write out the process, the chart is drawn for you. It’s useful for documenting procedures, training new team members, or just untangling a messy process visually.

Open Simple Flow →

AfterTaxTim

AfterTaxTim is a browser extension that reads salary figures on job listings and shows an estimate of the after-tax income. It is designed for people who want to see what a salary might actually mean in their bank account each month, without needing to open a separate calculator.

Extension link (coming soon)

Roster Mate

Roster Mate is a roster viewer that makes multi-week rosters easier to read. It can highlight pay days and show patterns over several weeks, so staff can see their shifts and key dates at a glance.

Open Roster Mate (coming soon)

CommuterCraig

CommuterCraig compares commute options and shows both driving and public transport times, including the steps involved (for example, walk → train → bus). It’s designed to help people quickly see which option makes the most sense for their day.

Open CommuterCraig (coming soon)

Home Time

Home Time is an idea for a browser extension that shows how long it would take to get home right now, based on live travel times and timetables. It’s aimed at people who finish work at different times and want a quick answer to “what time would I be home if I left now?”.

Currently in concept and early development.

Screen Drop

Screen Drop is a planned screenshot helper that would capture a screen, make it easy to share, and automatically delete the file after a short period. The idea is to keep things tidy while still making it simple to show someone exactly what you’re seeing.

Currently in concept and early development.
